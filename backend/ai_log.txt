### **Classic Biryani Recipe**

This recipe follows the "Dum" style of cooking, where the rice and meat are layered and slow-cooked to perfection.

#### **Ingredients:**

**For the Marinade:**
*   500g Chicken or Mutton (cut into pieces)
*   1 cup thick yogurt (curd)
*   1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic paste
*   1 tsp Turmeric powder
*   1 tsp Red chili powder
*   1 tbsp Biryani Masala powder
*   Salt to taste
*   A handful of chopped mint and coriander leaves

**For the Rice:**
*   2 cups Long-grain Basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
*   Whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 4 green cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 3-4 cloves
*   Salt (enough to make the water taste like seawater)

**For Layering & Garnish:**
*   2 large onions, thinly sliced and deep-fried until golden brown (Birista)
*   2 tbsp Ghee (clarified butter)
*   A pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (for the orange/yellow color)
*   Fresh coriander and mint leaves

---

#### **Instructions:**

1.  **Marinate the Meat:** In a large bowl, mix the meat with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, biryani masala, salt, and fresh herbs. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour (overnight is better).
2.  **Cook the Meat:** In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat some oil or ghee. Add the marinated meat and cook until it is about 80% done and the gravy has thickened. Set aside.
3.  **Prepare the Rice:** Boil a large pot of water with whole spices and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is about 70% done (the grain should still have a slight "bite" to it). Drain the water.
4.  **Layering:** 
    *   In a heavy pot, spread a layer of the cooked meat at the bottom. 
    *   Top it with half of the parboiled rice.
    *   Sprinkle half of the fried onions, coriander, mint, and a drizzle of ghee.
    *   Add the remaining rice as the final layer.
    *   Pour the saffron milk over the top to create those beautiful yellow and white rice patches seen in your image.
    *   Top with the rest of the fried onions and herbs.
5.  **The "Dum" (Steaming):** Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid (you can use aluminum foil or dough to seal the edges). Cook on very low heat for 15–20 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking in the steam.
6.  **Serve:** Gently fluff the rice with a fork, mixing the layers slightly. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salan, just like the side dish pictured!
